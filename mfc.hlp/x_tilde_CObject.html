  virtual ~CObject()
 
  Remarks
 
  This function is the standard CObject destructor. If your derived class
  must free allocated memory or do other cleanup work, you must provide
  your own destructor. Because ~CObject is a virtual destructor, C++
  ensures that CObject::~CObject is automatically called as part of the
  destructor of your class.
 
  NOTE: Your destructor should not throw exceptions or allocate objects.
